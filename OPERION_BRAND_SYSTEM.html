01The Mark

The Execution Vector


A precision circle — the orbit — enclosing a right-pointing equilateral triangle — the execution vector. The circle represents order, completeness, and operational precision. The triangle points unambiguously forward: not potential, but motion. Not aspiration, but execution.



Primary Mark
Dark badge. Default for all digital surfaces.
Light Mark
White badge. For print, light surfaces, inverted layouts.
Sigil
Bare mark, no container. Watermarks, large-format, embossing.
Sigil — Inverted
For light overlays and reversed watermarks.
02Construction Grid

Geometric Rationale


The mark is built on a single mathematical relationship: the golden section (φ ≈ 1.618). The inscribed circle of the triangle (r = 52) and the precision ring (r = 84) share the ratio 52 / 84 ≈ 0.619 ≈ 1/φ. This is not decorative — it is structural. The left edge of the triangle sits at a perfect vertical, creating a stable anchor.

04Color System

Strictly Monochromatic


OPERION uses no color. This is not a limitation — it is a statement. Monochromatic identity communicates that the work speaks for itself. Every element either is Carbon or White. There is no in-between.



Deep Carbon
#0D0D0D
Primary — backgrounds, containers, type on light. Not pure black: slightly warm, less harsh on screen.
Near White
#FAFAFA
Primary inverse — type on dark, mark strokes on dark badges, light surfaces.
Carbon Mid
#6B6B6B
Supporting — labels, captions, secondary text. Never used for headings or marks.
Off-White
#F5F5F5
Page backgrounds, card fills. Creates depth on all-white layouts without adding color.


Color Rules

RuleRationale
No accent colors — ever Color additions must be approved at the strategic level (e.g., entering a market that requires cultural color signaling). The mark itself is never colored.
#0D0D0D, not #000000 Pure black reads as harsh and flat on screens. Deep Carbon retains warmth and depth at scale.
#FAFAFA, not #FFFFFF Pure white on screen causes eye strain at scale. Near White maintains the monochromatic discipline without harshness.
Gradient: never Gradients imply transition and complexity. OPERION executes. No transitions.
Transparency only for hierarchy Opacity levels (40–60%) are permitted for tertiary text only. The mark is always full opacity.
05Typography

06Usage Rules

What to Do. What Never to Do.



Never

  • Add color to the mark or wordmark
  • Rotate the mark (the direction matters: it points right)
  • Stretch or skew the mark disproportionately
  • Place the mark on a patterned or photographic background without isolation
  • Use a font weight other than 200 for the wordmark
  • Apply drop shadows, glows, or bevels to the mark
  • Use the old OPERION geometry (upward triangle)
  • Reduce below minimum size (24px digital, 8mm print)
  • Change letter-spacing on the wordmark by more than ±2px
  • Add the "™" or "®" symbol until trademark is filed

Always

  • Use the correct variant for the surface (dark mark on light, light mark on dark)
  • Maintain clear space of 1× mark height on all sides
  • Use the bare sigil for watermarks (no badge container)
  • Scale the badge rx proportionally with the badge size
  • Use the horizontal lockup as the primary layout choice
  • Keep the mark and wordmark in the same color family (no mix)
  • Use ALL-CAPS for the wordmark without exception
  • Apply 10% opacity sigil for video watermarks (bottom-right corner)
  • Preserve stroke-width proportionality when scaling SVGs
  • Deliver SVG for digital, PDF/EPS for print

07Brand Evolution

From OPERION to OPERION


The OPERION mark was an inverted V — two lines rising to an amber apex. It pointed up: aspiration, emergence, potential. OPERION inherits the same equilateral triangle geometry, but rotates it 90°. It points right: motion, execution, operation.


This is not a redesign. It is a rotation. Same DNA. Transformed meaning.
