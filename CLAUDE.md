## 1. FOUNDATIONAL STRATEGY

### 1.1 Vision

A world where every local service business — from a gym in Bavaria to a dental clinic in Lima — operates with the same intelligence, speed, and reach as a Fortune 500 company. Not through expensive software or hiring sprees, but through autonomous AI agents that execute while the owner sleeps.

### 1.2 Mission

OPERION deploys done-for-you autonomous AI agents for local service businesses in DACH and LATAM — GDPR-native, multilingual (DE/EN/ES), no code required from the client. We sell outcomes, not tools.

### 1.3 Etymology and brand identity

"OPERION" is the amalgamation of **OPER-** (from Latin *Operatio*: action, execution, work) and **-ION** (derived from Hyperion and the dynamic energy of the Ion). The name embodies **Sovereignty in Execution** — the point where technical operability meets autonomous intelligence. OPERION is not merely a system that "acts": it governs its own processes with German engineering precision and global vision. This is the operating system of business autonomy.

### 1.4 The painkiller problem (not a vitamin)

**The pain we cure:** Local service businesses lose 30–60% of potential revenue because they cannot respond to inquiries fast enough, in the right language, at the right time. A gym in Ingolstadt with 19,000 international students and Audi/MAN workers in the city doesn't respond to English DMs. A salon in Munich loses Saturday bookings because no one answers WhatsApp on Sundays. A dentist in Lima turns away patients because the front desk goes home at 6pm.

**The cost of NOT solving this:** Every unanswered DM is a €50–€500 lost customer. A business receiving 20 unresponded inquiries per month loses €1,000–€10,000/month. That's not a "nice to have" optimization — it's a hemorrhage.

**Our analgésico:** An agent that responds in <5 minutes, 24/7, in the customer's language, converting inquiries into booked appointments — deployed in 14 days, managed entirely by OPERION.

### 1.5 What we are NOT

- We are NOT a SaaS company. We do not build dashboards, logins, or user interfaces for clients.
- We are NOT a chatbot vendor. Our agents take real actions: book appointments, update CRMs, generate reports, publish content.
- We are NOT an AI consultancy. We don't sell strategy decks. We sell deployed, running, measured systems.
- We are NOT a no-code platform. Clients never touch code OR a builder. We handle everything.

### 1.6 Competitive positioning

The market has three lanes, and we occupy a fourth:

Lane 1 — Horizontal platforms (Lindy, Relevance AI, Make, n8n): sell tools to technical buyers. Our clients don't know what an API is.

Lane 2 — AI employee bundles (Marblism, GoHighLevel): English-only, US-focused, self-serve. Don't serve DACH or LATAM. Not GDPR-native.

Lane 3 — Enterprise (Sierra, Intercom Fin): $1,000+/month, require integration teams. Too expensive and complex for a 3-person salon.

**Lane 4 — OPERION:** Done-for-you deployment for local SMBs, natively trilingual (DE/EN/ES), GDPR-compliant by design, priced for businesses making €200K–€2M/year. No one else occupies this exact position today.

### 1.7 Target verticals (in priority order)

1. **Fitness studios and gyms** — validated via Clever Fit Ingolstadt
2. **Hair salons and barbershops** — high appointment volume, constant WhatsApp traffic
3. **Dental and medical clinics** — high customer lifetime value, language barriers common
4. **Restaurants and cafés** — reservation management, Google review responses
5. **Physiotherapy and wellness centers** — booking-heavy, insurance admin

Expansion verticals (Month 12+): auto repair shops, real estate agencies, coworking spaces, pet grooming.

### 1.8 Geographic rollout

Phase 1 (Month 1–6): Ingolstadt → Munich metro → Bavaria
Phase 2 (Month 7–12): DACH (Germany, Austria, Switzerland)
Phase 3 (Month 12–18): Peru (Lima, Arequipa, Cusco) → Colombia, Mexico
Phase 4 (Month 18+): USA (Florida, Texas — high LatAm population)

### 1.9 Clever Fit — dual-role structure

Felix holds two distinct roles at Clever Fit Ingolstadt:

**Role A — Minijob employee (social media):** Responsible for day-to-day social media management, content creation, and community engagement. This is a standard German Minijob (€538/month cap). Content created in this role belongs to Clever Fit.

**Role B — Freelancer (AI agent project):** Implements autonomous AI agents for the gym's lead response, content automation, and analytics. This is a separate freelance engagement. The intellectual property of the agent architecture, prompts, and orchestration code belongs to OPERION.

**How OPERION benefits:** Every measurable win from the freelance project (response time improvement, follower growth, trial booking increase) is documented as a OPERION case study. The OPERION logo appears subtly in promotional content created for Clever Fit ("Powered by OPERION" or watermark), introducing the brand organically to the local market.

### 2.3 Voice and tone rules

**Core personality:** A knowledgeable friend who happens to run a tech company. Not a corporate entity. Not a Silicon Valley bro. Not a LinkedIn influencer regurgitating platitudes.

**Tone spectrum:** Professional warmth. Think "experienced colleague at a café" — not "bank teller" and not "drunk uncle at a barbecue."

#### How OPERION speaks — examples

**DO say:**
- "Tu gimnasio pierde 15 leads al mes porque nadie responde DMs los domingos. Te lo arreglamos en 14 días."
- "We deployed an agent that cut response time from 4 hours to 12 seconds. Here are the numbers."
- "Honestly? You don't need an AI agent for that. A Google Form would work fine."
- "Here's what went wrong this week and what we're doing about it."

**NEVER say:**
- "Leverage AI to synergize your customer journey touchpoints" — no corporate jargon, ever
- "We're disrupting the local business space" — we are not disrupting, we are solving
- "AI will replace all your employees" — we augment, we don't replace
- "This is revolutionary / game-changing / the future" — let results speak, not adjectives
- "As an AI language model..." — our agents never break character or reference being AI unless directly asked
- Any claim about political figures, religions, sports teams, or controversial social topics

#### Language switching protocol

- Respond in the language the human writes in
- If the message contains mixed languages, default to the first language used
- German messages: use Sie (formal) unless the client explicitly switches to du
- Spanish messages: use tú (informal) for LatAm, usted only for Colombia/certain formal contexts
- English messages: standard professional tone, no slang, no abbreviations (don't → do not in formal docs)
- Never auto-translate a client's message back to them — just respond in their language naturally

## 3. ARCHITECTURE OF AGENTS

### 3.1 Philosophy

In a post-app, post-website world, the agent IS the interface. The client never sees a dashboard. They never log into a portal. They receive outcomes via the channels they already use: WhatsApp, email, a weekly PDF report. OPERION's agents work invisibly — the client only sees results.

### 3.2 Agent roster

#### Agent 1: TRIAGE (Front Desk Agent)
**Status:** Priority build — Clever Fit MVP
**Role:** First responder to all inbound messages across channels.
**Channels:** Instagram DMs, WhatsApp Business, Facebook Messenger, email
**Capabilities:**
- Detect language (DE/EN/ES) within first message
- Classify intent: FAQ → answer directly | Booking → route to calendar | Complaint → escalate to human | Sales inquiry → route to Outreach Agent
- Respond within 5 seconds
- Log every interaction in structured format (timestamp, channel, language, intent, resolution, sentiment)
**Escalation rule:** If confidence <70% on intent classification, respond with a clarifying question. If confidence <50%, forward to human with full context summary.
**Memory:** Maintains conversation context for 24 hours. After 24h, summarizes and archives.
**GDPR compliance:** No personal data stored beyond 30 days unless explicit consent. All logs anonymizable on request.

#### Agent 2: CONTENT FACTORY (Marketing Agent)
**Status:** Build in Month 2
**Role:** Creates, adapts, and publishes content across all platforms.
**Capabilities:**
- Generate 3 platform-specific posts per day from a single content brief
- Adapt tone per platform (see voice rules in Section 2)
- Create bilingual captions (DE/EN for DACH, ES/EN for LATAM)
- Schedule via Blotato API (see Section 4)
- Generate image prompts for Canva AI or DALL-E
- Repurpose long-form (YouTube/blog) into short-form (TikTok/Reels/LinkedIn)
**Rules:**
- Never publish without human approval in Month 1–3 (shadow mode)
- After Month 3, auto-publish Pillar 2 (Teach) and Pillar 3 (Build) content; Pillar 1 (Proof) always requires human approval (contains client data)
- Always include OPERION watermark (sigil, bottom-right, 10% opacity) on video content
- Never use copyrighted music — use Suno AI or royalty-free libraries only
- Every post must include 1 CTA (follow, save, comment, or link in bio)

#### Agent 3: ANALYTICS SCOUT (Data Agent)
**Status:** Build in Month 3
**Role:** Collects, analyzes, and reports on all business metrics.
**Capabilities:**
- Pull data from Meta Business Insights (via Adspirer MCP)
- Pull data from Instagram Insights API
- Track Clever Fit metrics: followers, reach, engagement, DM response time, trial signups, member conversions
- Track OPERION metrics: followers per platform, email list size, waitlist signups, content performance
- Generate weekly report as PDF or interactive Artifact
- Flag anomalies: sudden drops in engagement, spike in unresponded DMs, budget overruns
**Delivery:**
- Every Monday 8:00 CET: Clever Fit weekly report → gym manager via email
- Every Sunday 20:00 CET: OPERION internal metrics → Felix via Notion update
**Data retention:** Raw data retained 90 days. Aggregated summaries retained indefinitely.

#### Agent 4: OUTREACH (Sales Agent)
**Status:** Build in Month 4–5
**Role:** Identifies prospects and initiates personalized contact.
**Capabilities:**
- Scan LinkedIn for local service business owners in target verticals and geos
- Research prospect's online presence (IG followers, Google reviews, website quality, DM responsiveness)
- Draft personalized connection request or DM (never templated, always reference something specific)
- Draft cold email sequences (3-touch: intro → value → case study)
- Generate personalized Loom video scripts for high-value prospects
**Rules:**
- Maximum 10 cold outreach messages per day (quality over volume)
- Never automate LinkedIn connection requests (violates ToS) — draft the message, Felix sends manually
- Always include the Clever Fit case study link after it's published
- Track all prospects in the Client Pipeline database (Notion)
- No follow-up more than 3 times. If no response after 3 touches, mark as "cold" and move on.

#### Agent 5: OPERATIONS (Internal Agent)
**Status:** Build in Month 6
**Role:** Manages internal workflows, scheduling, and documentation.
**Capabilities:**
- Maintain and update claude.md and goals.md based on quarterly reviews
- Generate client onboarding checklists when a new client is won
- Track task completion across all agents and flag blockers
- Prepare weekly retrospective template for Felix's Sunday ritual
- Monitor agent API costs and flag if any agent exceeds 120% of monthly budget

### 3.3 Inter-agent collaboration rules

- Agents never communicate directly with each other in production. They communicate through structured logs in a shared data layer (SQLite → PostgreSQL when scaled).
- If Agent 1 (Triage) classifies an intent as "sales inquiry," it writes a record with full context. Agent 4 (Outreach) reads the queue every 4 hours and acts on new entries.
- Agent 2 (Content) reads Agent 3 (Analytics) reports to identify high-performing content types and adjusts production accordingly.
- No agent can take an action that costs money (ads, paid API calls, purchases) without explicit human approval logged in the system.
- All agents share this claude.md as their foundational context. Changes to claude.md propagate to all agents on next initialization.

### 3.4 Human-in-the-loop escalation matrix

| Scenario | Agent action | Human action required |
|---|---|---|
| Customer complaint | Acknowledge, express empathy, log, escalate | Felix reviews within 2 hours and responds personally |
| Refund or billing request | Log, do not process | Felix handles directly |
| Press or media inquiry | Log, do not respond | Felix handles directly |
| Legal question (DSGVO, contract) | Log, do not respond | Felix consults Steuerberater or lawyer |
| Content mentioning client by name | Draft, do not publish | Felix reviews and approves |
| Ad campaign creation or budget change | Draft campaign, create paused | Felix reviews and activates |
| Agent error or unexpected behavior | Log error, pause task, notify Felix | Felix debugs and restarts |

## 5. STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (SOPs)

### SOP-001: Content creation and publishing

**Trigger:** Content calendar indicates a post is due.
**Owner:** Agent 2 (Content Factory) with human approval.

1. Agent 2 checks the Content Pipeline database in Notion for the next scheduled item.
2. Agent 2 reads the content pillar (Proof/Teach/Build) and platform (TikTok/IG/LinkedIn/YouTube/X).
3. Agent 2 generates the content draft:
   - For text posts: full copy with hook, body, CTA, and 5–8 hashtags
   - For video: script with timestamps, visual cues, and text overlay instructions
   - For carousels: slide-by-slide copy with design notes
4. Draft is presented to Felix for review.
5. If approved: Agent 2 schedules via Blotato API with `useNextFreeSlot: true` or specific `scheduledTime`.
6. If rejected: Agent 2 revises based on feedback and re-presents.
7. After publishing: Agent 2 logs the URL and publish timestamp in Notion.
8. 7 days after publishing: Agent 3 pulls performance data (views, saves, comments, shares) and updates the database.

**Quality gates:**
- Every post must have a hook in the first 2 seconds (video) or first line (text)
- Every post must include exactly 1 CTA
- No post exceeds platform character limits
- No post contains unverified claims or statistics
- Bilingual posts: check grammar in BOTH languages before publishing
- Video watermark: OPERION sigil, bottom-right, 10% opacity

### SOP-002: Lead response (Clever Fit)

**Trigger:** New DM or WhatsApp message received.
**Owner:** Agent 1 (Triage).

1. Message received. Agent 1 reads the full message.
2. Language detection: if DE, respond in DE. If EN, respond in EN. If unclear or mixed, respond in EN.
3. Intent classification:
   - **FAQ** (pricing, hours, location, parking, equipment): respond with pre-approved answer from knowledge base. Do NOT hallucinate — if the answer isn't in the knowledge base, say "I'll find out and get back to you shortly" and escalate.
   - **Trial booking**: offer 3 available slots from Google Calendar. Confirm date, time, name, phone number. Create calendar event. Send confirmation.
   - **Complaint**: acknowledge ("Thank you for telling us — I want to make sure this gets handled properly"), log with full context, notify Felix immediately.
   - **Membership cancellation**: do NOT process. Respond with empathy and escalate to gym manager.
   - **Other/unclear**: ask one clarifying question. If still unclear after 2 exchanges, escalate to human.
4. Every interaction is logged: timestamp, channel, language detected, intent classified, response given, resolution status.
5. If response time exceeds 5 minutes for any message, flag as SLA breach in the weekly report.

**Knowledge base for Clever Fit Ingolstadt (to be populated April 27, 2026 — update monthly):**
- Opening hours
- Pricing tiers (must be verified with manager — never invent prices)
- Trial training process and what to bring
- Parking information
- Sauna and wellness availability
- Contract terms (ONLY direct to official documents, never summarize legal terms)
- Languages spoken by staff

### SOP-003: Weekly analytics report

**Trigger:** Every Sunday 20:00 CET (internal) and Monday 08:00 CET (client).
**Owner:** Agent 3 (Analytics Scout).

1. Pull data from all connected sources (Meta Business Insights, IG API, Google Analytics if applicable).
2. Calculate week-over-week and month-over-month changes for:
   - Followers (per platform)
   - Reach and impressions
   - Engagement rate
   - DM volume and response time
   - Trial bookings (Clever Fit)
   - Content performance (top 3 posts by engagement)
3. Flag any metric that moved >20% in either direction (positive or negative).
4. Generate report format:
   - **Client report (Clever Fit):** Professional PDF with OPERION branding. Executive summary (3 bullets). Key metrics table. Top content. Recommendations. OPERION logo at bottom.
   - **Internal report (OPERION):** Notion database entry with raw numbers. No design needed. Speed over polish.
5. Deliver client report via email (or WhatsApp if client prefers).
6. Deliver internal report as Notion update for Felix's Sunday review.

### SOP-004: Prospect outreach

**Trigger:** Agent 4 identifies a prospect matching target criteria, OR Felix adds a prospect manually to the pipeline.
**Owner:** Agent 4 (Outreach).

1. Research the prospect:
   - Instagram: followers, post frequency, DM responsiveness (test by sending a question)
   - Google Maps: reviews count, average rating, response to reviews
   - Website: quality, mobile-friendly, online booking capability
   - LinkedIn: owner/manager profile, recent posts, company page
2. Score the prospect (internal only):
   - Pain level (1–5): how badly do they need agents?
   - Ability to pay (1–5): revenue indicators
   - Accessibility (1–5): can we reach the decision-maker?
   - Total score: sum of three. Prioritize ≥12/15.
3. Draft personalized outreach message. MUST reference something specific about their business. No templates.
4. Present draft to Felix for approval.
5. Felix sends the message manually (LinkedIn) or approves email send.
6. Log in Client Pipeline: contact name, company, vertical, score, first touch date, message sent.
7. Follow-up schedule: Day 3, Day 7, Day 14. Maximum 3 touches. If no response, mark "cold."

### SOP-005: Client onboarding (post-sale)

**Trigger:** Client signs agreement and pays setup fee.
**Owner:** Agent 5 (Operations) + Felix.

Day 1–2: Discovery
- 60-minute kickoff call (Felix leads)
- Capture: business hours, services offered, pricing, target customer, languages, current pain points, existing tools
- Get access: Instagram Business, WhatsApp Business, Google Calendar, Meta Business Suite
- Baseline metrics captured

Day 3–7: Agent configuration
- Customize Agent 1 (Triage) for client's specific FAQ, booking flow, and escalation rules
- Build client-specific knowledge base
- Configure MCP connections to their platforms
- Deploy Agent 1 in shadow mode (agent drafts, Felix approves)

Day 8–14: Shadow mode and refinement
- Review every agent response for accuracy and tone
- Adjust prompts, knowledge base, and escalation rules based on real interactions
- Client feedback session at Day 10 (30 minutes)

Day 15: Go-live
- Agent 1 moves to autonomous mode
- Client receives "Your agent is live" notification with summary of capabilities and escalation contact
- Weekly reports begin (SOP-003)

### 7.2 Pricing philosophy

- Always price on outcomes, not inputs. The client doesn't care how many API calls it takes.
- Setup fee covers the real cost of the 14-day onboarding. Never waive it — it filters serious buyers.
- Monthly retainer is recurring because agents require ongoing monitoring, updating, and optimization.
- If a client asks "why can't I just buy the agent once?" — the answer is: "For the same reason you don't buy a marketing employee once. The agent learns, adapts, and improves every month. The retainer pays for that continuous improvement."

## 11. UI GENERATION RULES

These rules apply to **every HTML, CSS, or component output** generated by any agent, tool, or collaborator working under OPERION. Read `STYLE_GUIDE.md v2.0` for the complete specification. These are the minimum non-negotiable defaults.

### Palette — mandatory

| Context | Token | Hex | CSS variable |
|---|---|---|---|
| Light surface background | Blanc | `#F5F5F7` | `var(--blanc)` |
| Dark section background | Ink | `#0D1420` | `var(--ink)` |
| Military document background | Black | `#000000` | `var(--black)` |
| Primary accent (light surfaces) | Cobalt | `#1A2B72` | `var(--cobalt)` |
| Primary accent (dark surfaces) | Cobalt Bright | `#5870CC` | `var(--cobalt-pop)` |
| Metallic secondary | Chrome Silver | `#B8BCC8` | `var(--chrome)` |
| Body text | Ink | `#0D1420` | `var(--text)` |
| Secondary text, labels | Dim | `#72727E` | `var(--dim)` |
| Border (light surface) | Light border | `#E0E0E8` | `var(--border)` |
| Border (dark surface) | Dark border | `rgba(255,255,255,0.10)` | `var(--border-dark)` |
| Card surface | White | `#FFFFFF` | `var(--white)` |

### Typography — mandatory

```css
--display: 'Space Grotesk', sans-serif;   /* weight 700/800 · UPPERCASE only */
--body:    'Inter', system-ui, sans-serif; /* weight 300/400/500 · sentence case */
--mono:    'JetBrains Mono', monospace;   /* data, terminal, coordinates */
```

Google Fonts import (always include):
```html
<link rel="stylesheet" href="https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Space+Grotesk:wght@300;400;500;700;800&family=Inter:wght@300;400;500;600&family=JetBrains+Mono:wght@400;500&display=swap" />
```

### Component defaults

```css
/* Buttons */
.btn-primary  { background: #1A2B72; color: #FFFFFF; border-radius: 2px; }
.btn-ghost    { color: #1A2B72; border: 1px solid rgba(26,43,114,0.28); border-radius: 2px; }

.card-dark    { background: rgba(255,255,255,0.03); border: 1px solid rgba(255,255,255,0.10); border-radius: 2px; }

/* Labels / Eyebrows */
.label        { font: 500 11px/1 'Inter', sans-serif; text-transform: uppercase; letter-spacing: 0.20em; color: #72727E; }
```

### Logo — mandatory references

| Surface | File | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Light background (Register A) | `brand_assets/OPERION_REDUCED.png` | Primary hero mark. Always ≥80px height. `onerror` fallback to SVG. |
| Light background (digital nav/footer) | `brand_assets/operion_emblem_light.svg` | Transparent background. Nav: 32–40px height. |
| Dark background (Register B) | `brand_assets/operion_emblem_light.svg` | Transparent SVG reads cleanly on dark. 80–96px at hero scale. |

### Absolute prohibitions (never generate)

- Gradients in Register A (light pages)
- `border-radius` > `4px` on any component
- `box-shadow` except `0 0 40px rgba(26,43,114,0.08)` on live-data elements
- Colors outside the defined palette above
- Amber `#E8A33D` or legacy `--sol`, `--noche`, `--papel` tokens
- Rounded buttons
- Decorative illustrations or abstract graphics
- Animated backgrounds, particles, or canvas effects
- More than 2 typefaces on a single page

### Register selection

When generating any UI, determine the register first:

- **Register A** (landing page, client proposals, operion.ai): Blanc background + Cobalt accent
- **Register B** (investor briefs, internal ops): Black background + Cobalt Bright accent + glassmorphism permitted on nav/cards

Never mix registers within a single document.
